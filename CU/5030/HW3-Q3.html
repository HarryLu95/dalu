a

Create 1000 new data sets each with the values of \(log(G)\) and \(log(W)\) using the coefficients and residual standard error estimated in Question 2 to produce the values in \(y\) (you will need to re-load the data and re-run lm to get this). On each data set, conduct an linear regression and record the estimated \(\hat{\beta}\) its standard error, and \(\hat{\sigma}\).

brain_data=read.table("https://raw.githubusercontent.com/HarryLu95/dalu/master/CU/5030/BrainWeight.txt",head=TRUE)
#colnames(brain_data)=c("B","W","G")
brain_data$ln_B=log(brain_data$Brain,base=exp(1))
brain_data$ln_W=log(brain_data$Body,base=exp(1))
brain_data$ln_G=log(brain_data$Gestation,base=exp(1))
brain_mod=lm(ln_B~ln_W+ln_G,data=brain_data)
sigma=summary(brain_mod)$sigma
set.seed(1)
B=matrix(0,nrow=1000,ncol=10)
for (i in 1:1000){
  temp_y=brain_mod$fitted.values+rnorm(96,mean=0,sd=sigma)
  temp_summary=summary(lm(temp_y~brain_data$ln_W+brain_data$ln_G))
  temp_beta0=temp_summary$coefficients[1,1]
  temp_se_beta0=temp_summary$coefficients[1,2]
  temp_var_beta0=temp_se_beta0^2
  temp_beta1=temp_summary$coefficients[2,1]
  temp_se_beta1=temp_summary$coefficients[2,2]
  temp_var_beta1=temp_se_beta1^2
  temp_beta2=temp_summary$coefficients[3,1]
  temp_se_beta2=temp_summary$coefficients[3,2]
  temp_var_beta2=temp_se_beta2^2
  temp_sigma=temp_summary$sigma
  B[i,]=c(temp_beta0,temp_se_beta0,temp_var_beta0,temp_beta1,temp_se_beta1,temp_var_beta1,temp_beta2,temp_se_beta2,temp_var_beta2,temp_sigma)
}
rm(i,temp_y,temp_summary,temp_beta0,temp_se_beta0,temp_var_beta0,temp_beta1,temp_se_beta1,temp_var_beta1,temp_beta2,temp_se_beta2,temp_var_beta2,temp_sigma)
colnames(B)=c("beta0","se_beta0","var_beta0","beta1","se_beta1","var_beta1","beta2","se_beta2","var_beta2","sigma")

f

Construct a histogram of the 1000 chi-squared statistics of the form

\[\chi^2 = f * \frac{\hat{\sigma}^2_i}{\sigma^2}\]

where \(\sigma^2\) is the variance used to generate your errors, and f is the error degrees of freedom. Overlay a chi-squared density with f degrees of freedom.

  • \(\widehat{\sigma^2}_i\) -> d$sigma^2
  • \(\sigma^2\) -> approximately equal to the mean of d$sigma^2
  • choose \(f\) as 93

As the above says, degree of freedom should be 93.

Chi_s=c()
sigma_square=mean((d$sigma)^2)
for (i in 1:1000){
  Chi_s[i]=93*(d$sigma[i])^2/sigma_square
}
rm(i)
hist(Chi_s,breaks=50,probability=TRUE)
curve(dchisq(x,93),add=T)
